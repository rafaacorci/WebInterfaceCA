Fixed gear bikes: A ride back to simplicity

Fixed gear bikes, often fondly called fixies are as minimalist as a bicycle can get. Unlike modern bicycle, fixed gear bikes have no freehubs mechanisms. This means when the wheels turn, so does the pedals. Therefore, there is no coasting when riding them, only pure connection between the rider and the machine.

A brief history

The roots of fixed gear bikes can be traced to the early days of cycling in the 19th century. Before derailleurs, gears, and multi speed cassettes became the norm, all bikes were in essence fixed gear bikes. They are reliable, simple and easy to maintain. These characteristics have led to a modern day resurgence.

Track cyccling brought fixed gear bikes into the competitive scene, with velodrome riders embracing the fixed gear for its efficienncy and responsiveness. Over time, urban cyclists, specially bike messengers, adopted the fixed gear bike for their low maintenance needs and the raw responsive riding experience they offer.

Why ride fixies?

Riding a fixed gear bike is all about control, rhythm, and feel. With fewer components, there's less weight and less that can go wrong. Many riders say that riding fixed improves their cadence, handling, and connection to the road. Plus, in busy cities, the simplicity of a fixie means more time riding and less time wrenching. Whether fixies drawn you due to its elegant lines, tight feedback from the drivertain, or nostalgia to cycling's golden age, fixed gear bikes offer a unique experience that isn't easily replicated.
